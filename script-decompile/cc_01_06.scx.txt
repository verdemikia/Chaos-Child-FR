[center][margin top="242"]Half-past midnight.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
[name]Man[line]“...”[%p]
[name]Policeman[line]“Shinjo-san, we’ve managed to ID the victim from his possessions and the security cameras.”[%p]
[name]Policeman[line]“His name is Kakita Hironori. Twenty-two years old. A Shibuya resident with a part time marketing job at [color index="280AA02D1400"]SafeWorks[color index="800000"].”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“A part-timer at SafeWorks? Any lovers?”[%p]
[name]Policeman[line]“None. No roommates, either. He lived alone, it seems.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Shit.”[%p]
Shinjo cursed.[%p]
If he was single and not in a relationship, it would take time to figure out his private life.[%p]
And SafeWorks was one of the companies that had profited the most from the recovery projects in recent years. Part-timer or not, if he worked there, he was probably pretty busy.[%p]
It was possible that his co-workers knew just his name and nothing else about who he was outside of work.[%p]
[name]Policeman[line]“Shinjo-san, what do we tell the media? Kaiyomi TV and Niconiya’s net reporters are refusing to leave...”[%p]
[name]Policeman[line]“And those two kids we found at the scene, who said they’re with the Hekiho Newspaper Club... They’ve calmed down, but they’re still not making a lot of sense.”[%p]
[name]Policeman[line]“That other girl doesn’t have anything that would tell us who she is... We’re checking if she’s a friend or relative of Kakita’s, but no luck yet. She’s refusing to say anything.”[%p]
[name]Policeman[line]“Also, forensics wanted to know—”[%p]
[name]Policeman[line]“Hey! You can’t just—”[%p]
[name]Watabe[line]“This is Watabe from [color index="280AA02D1400"]Niconiya News[color index="800000"]! Excuse me, what’s going on here—”[%p]
[name]Policeman[line]“Get out! You can’t come in here without permission!”[%p]
[name]Watabe[line]“Just a brief comment! A brief comment is all I need!”[%p]
Shinjo’s men physically dragged the reporter out of the room.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
He had to stop himself from putting his hands to his head and moaning.[%p]
He couldn’t let his men see him acting weak.[%p]
Shinjo had been with Police HQ’s Section 1 for less than seven years. He didn’t get along with his superiors, but he’d managed to beg his way into being in charge of this case.[%p]
Maybe this was his way of avenging the death of an older detective who he’d admired.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Once you’re done looking at the window frame, make sure it can’t be seen from the outside. There’s no telling what those net reporters will do.”[%p]
[name]Policeman[line]“Yes, sir!”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“I’ll talk to forensics myself. Oh, and put some more manpower into checking his connections at work. I’ll tell the bosses.”[%p]
[name]Policeman[line]“Yes, sir! So what about those kids?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“I’ll handle them, too. Get to it.”[%p]
[name]Policeman[line]“Yes, sir!”[linebreak][parallel]“Yes, sir!”[%p]
His men raced out of the room.[%p]
They’d never had a case like this before, and they were probably pretty excited.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
So was Shinjo, to be honest.[%p]
The whole thing was such a mess that he didn’t even know where to begin. But his detective’s instincts were screaming at him to start doing the work needed to find the killer.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...Okay.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
[name]Woman[line]“Hello, this is the Freesia Credit Bureau.”[%p]
His call was answered on the first ring, like always.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“It’s me.”[%p]
[name]Woman[line]“Oh, Shinjo-chan! You’re late. I thought you’d be contacting me earlier.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“I’m sorry.”[%p]
Shinjo apologized without thinking, and for no clear reason.[%p]
He always felt like a naughty child when he spoke to this CEO, who happened to be a woman.[%p]
[name]Woman[line]“You know why you’re so exhausted? It’s ’cause you’re taking this so seriously. You should relax a bit, like they did.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“I’ve got no intention of copying their habits.”[%p]
[name]Woman[line]“You’re so serious, aren’t you? If you keep on thinking that’s a virtue, you’re going to have a bad time of it one day.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Um, Momose-san... There’s something I wanted to talk to you about.”[%p]
Shinjo cut her off before she could continue her lecture and gave her a brief description of the scene.[%p]
This was a violation of the rules, but the only way to solve a crazy case was to be a little crazy yourself.[%p]
That was the first thing he’d learned from that older detective.[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“So is it murder? Or suicide?”[%p]
Momose asked her question after he’d described the situation.[%p]
She cut to the heart of the problem.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“We’re not sure right now.”[%p]
The hotel had gotten suspicious when Kakita had never come down after checkout time. When more than ten calls to the room went unanswered, they’d contacted the police.[%p]
Two officers, as well as someone from the hotel, had gone to the room, where they found the body and an unconscious girl.[%p]
One officer stayed at the scene to keep it secure, and the other searched the hotel to make sure it was safe.[%p]
During the search, he’d forgotten to lock the employee entrance in the back near the stairs. That was how the two students from Hekiho had gotten in.[%p]
Shortly after he’d left the room, the girl woke up and started strangling the officer who’d stayed behind. He’d hit his head on the edge of the glass window and knocked himself out.[%p]
He was going to be fine, but he’d been out cold until backup arrived.[%p]
When the backup got there, they found the girl unconscious on the floor. A boy from Hekiho was passed out on the floor, too.[%p]
The other girl from Hekiho was throwing up, also on the floor.[%p]
Kakita’s cause of death was asphyxia via strangulation.[%p]
The wire around his neck was a kind used at construction sites. It had been hung in an X pattern from the lights on the ceiling.[%p]
And he’d been sitting on the bed as it rotated.[%p]
That’s why there was so much pressure on his neck that finally, his head had been severed.[%p]
From the estimated time of death, Kakita had passed away before the police had even been contacted.[%p]
The body on the bed kept spinning in time to the music, one rotation every two minutes. In the end, the man’s head had been cut off completely.[%p]
The problem was the girl in the room.[%p]
There was no indication that she’d ever touched Kakita.[%p]
There were no fingerprints or any other signs that she’d touched Kakita’s body, the bed, or the wire.[%p]
The only things they’d found under her fingernails were skin and blood from the officer she’d strangled.[%p]
In fact, there was no indication she’d touched much of anything in the room.[%p]
Everything they found belonged to Kakita.[%p]
And according to the officer she’d strangled, when she attacked him, she had clearly been in an unstable psychological state.[%p]
Her eyes had been bloodshot and out of focus.[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“So what are you trying to say here? This girl just watched Kakita die without touching anything in the room, and then started strangling a police officer while temporarily insane?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
Shinjo scratched his head as he heard her sum up the situation.[%p]
That was what it looked like, at least from what they knew right now.[%p]
And just going by that, it sure looked like a suicide.[%p]
But it was all far too strange.[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“Hmm... I see. This is a bit of a problem. What about these kids from Hekiho that got inside the room?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“It seems like it was pretty traumatic for them, so at first they wouldn’t say anything. But now they’re starting to talk.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“But they’re from Hekiho, and that place has some issues.”[%p]
He’d checked with the school and verified that they were part of the school’s Newspaper Club.[%p]
The security camera at the rear entrance had seen them coming in, which matched up with their testimony.[%p]
But he didn’t know what to make of their testimony that the door had opened on its own, or that someone else had tried to come into the room.[%p]
The door was equipped with an automatic lock, and the only person inside the room who could’ve opened it was that girl.[%p]
But there was no sign of her fingerprints on the door.[%p]
And the security camera in the hallway showed no signs of anyone else trying to go in.[%p]
In any event, it was extremely rare for a civilian to see a dead body outside of a funeral.[%p]
And in an unstable mental state, it would be extremely easy for them to imagine things or just outright be mistaken about what was going on.[%p]
If they turned out to not have any relationship to Kakita or the girl, he planned to let them off with a stern warning.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Our biggest lead is the girl who was in the room, but she’s not talking.”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“She’s probably going to be put under [color index="280AA02D1400"]psychiatric hold[color index="800000"] at this rate, right?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Yeah. But that will slow down the investigation a lot, so I’d rather not go that route.”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“Got it. Let me get some information spread around. Given your position, this is probably going to be pretty rough for you. So be ready, okay? I’ll call back in three minutes.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Okay... Thanks. I appreciate it.”[%p]
Shinjo went to hang up the phone.[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“Oh, wait!”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Yeah?”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“Actually, I want you to do something for me. I’m sure it’ll be a big help for you, too. Can you do it?”[%p]
So it was basically an order, then, Shinjo thought to himself.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Hm. What is it?”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“One of my kids knows more about this than I do. And they say they want to see the crime scene personally. Can you help them out?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“You want me to let an outsider into a crime scene?”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“It’s always outsiders who’ll give you the hints you need to solve the case.”[%p]
“After all, I’m an outsider too,” she continued.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
In his head, Shinjo went over the steps he’d have to take and the time required to take them.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Can it be tomorrow... Or no, the day after?”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“That’s my Shinjo-chan! Glad we could work this out. I don’t know how you manage around the rest of those hardheads in Section 1.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“All right, talk to you later.”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“Hold on a second, Shinjo-chan, would you?”[%p]
He barely stopped himself from ending the call.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Is there something else?”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“I wanted to give you some advice, just in case. Two things.”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“First, what I said before wasn’t a joke, okay?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Huh?”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“You need to relax a little. Don’t take everything so seriously.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...?”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“I’m sure a lot of things are about to happen that will be beyond your understanding. When that happens, your worst enemy is what you think you know.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“I’ll make sure to remember that.”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“If that was a serious answer, you’ve still got a long ways to go.”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“And the other thing is...”[%p]
Momose paused for a second and chuckled.[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“Don’t let my kid get the better of you too often. If you let your guard down, you’ll end up an outsider yourself.”[%p]
