[center][margin top="242"]October 6th, 2015. Tuesday.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
[center][margin top="242"]9:00 PM.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%p]
[center]In front of Shibuya Station.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
The place was as crowded and noisy as ever.[%p]
The footsteps of people rushing to the station to get home after work. The voices of people talking into their phones about where to meet for drinks. The screams of joy as old friends were reunited...[%p]
It all blended together, and it all had an effect on anyone who passed through it, even for a moment.[%p]
Six years after the Shibuya Earthquake, the city had been rebuilt to the point where there was nothing left to remind you of it.[%p]
Everything was up and running again. Until a few years ago, many people would stop and stare in wonder at how everything was new, but not anymore.[%p]
The slogan of the restoration efforts, “Shibuya, a City Reborn,” had such an effect on people that the past was beginning to be forgotten.[%p]
At least, for people who weren’t in Shibuya during the earthquake.[%p]
Just a one-minute walk away from the station...[%p]
[center][margin top="242"]...was the Shibuya Earthquake Memorial.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
[name]Kurusu[line]“...”[%p]
Kurusu made sure to always visit once a week.[%p]
She would put a flower down by the name of her best friend from elementary school and pray silently.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“...”[%p]
It was only a short distance from the heart of the city, but the noise and commotion didn’t reach this place.[%p]
This place was somberly constructed, and pure, as if it wanted to be separated from all that.[%p]
Today was October 6th.[%p]
The earthquake was on November 6th, so this was the same day of the month.[%p]
Maybe that’s why there were so many flowers.[%p]
Some of them were lying scattered on the ground.[%p]
There was no one around.[%p]
Sometimes people would come at around 10:30 PM, when the earthquake struck, but other than that, the place was mostly empty at night.[%p]
Kurusu went around the area, grabbing up all the scattered flowers she could see and putting them on a nearby dais.[%p]
She’d done it many times before. It was all part of her routine.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“...What should I do?”[%p]
Kurusu was thinking about what had happened a few days before, and about her younger brother, Miyashiro.[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“You’re going to be killed, Miyashiro Takuru-senpai.”[auto-forward][linebreak][linebreak]
[name]Kurusu[line]“...”[%p]
She’d heard it clearly.[%p]
Arimura Hinae had said those words to Miyashiro.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“Takuru...”[%p]
No, that was impossible.[%p]
There was no way her brother was about to get caught up in something just because of what he’d learned—[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“Ahh...”[%p]
A sudden wind blew the flowers out of her hand.[%p]
They scattered away from her.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“...”[%p]
All she could do was watch.[%p]
For a moment, she tried to reach out and grab them, but her hand clawed uselessly at the air.[%p]
Before the feelings overwhelmed her, she turned toward the place where her friend’s name was carved.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“Hey.”[%p]
She rubbed it gently.[%p]
The familiar sensation came to her through her fingers.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“What do I do?”[%p]
But the name, Minamisawa Senri, just echoed the question back to her.[%p]
