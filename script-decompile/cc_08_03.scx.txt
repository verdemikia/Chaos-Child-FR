[center][margin top="242"]October 24th, 2015. Saturday.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
[name]Shinjo[line](I know this smell...)[%p]
That was the first thing Shinjo thought as he ducked under the crime scene tape and went inside the third floor condo.[%p]
He knew this smell.[%p]
But from where?[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](Oh, that’s right! New York!)[%p]
When he was training in America, a local colleague had taken him to a cheap steakhouse.[%p]
The waiter had brought him a disgusting grilled lamb steak.[%p]
The smell was a mix of rotting animal blood and burnt fat. He hadn’t even taken a single bite.[%p]
And that was precisely the smell that filled the room right now.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
The source of the stench was in the middle of the room, on top of the dinner table.[%p]
The forensics people must’ve finished their job, because it already had a sheet placed over it. Now, it was just waiting to be carried out to the coroner.[%p]
Shinjo put his hands together for a moment in silent prayer, and then flipped up the sheet.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Aah?!”[%p]
The second he saw it, he yanked the handkerchief out of his pocket and forced it to his mouth to stop himself from throwing up.[%p]
He knew it was disrespectful to the dead, but he couldn’t help it.[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Good evening, sir.”[%p]
He looked up and saw a younger detective talking to the forensics people.[%p]
The detective lacked his usual energy. This was probably the worst case he’d ever seen.[%p]
Shinjo didn’t want to look bad in front of the younger kid, so he forced the handkerchief away from his mouth.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Yeah, good evening. Have we identified the victim yet?”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Well, the body’s in bad shape, and she’s not wearing clothes. All we know for sure right now is that she’s a woman, somewhere between her teens and thirties.”[%p]
He could see hints on the charred flesh that indicated the body had once belonged to a woman.[%p]
But the face was so burnt that there was no way to distinguish any features.[%p]
And what was even stranger, was that it looked like a metal stick coming out of her mouth.[%p]
The rod came out all the way below her abdomen, like a skewer used to cook a kebab.[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“About ten days ago, we got a report of a piece of rebar being stolen from a nearby construction site.”[%p]
The detective flipped open his notepad.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Rebar? You mean those steel bars used to reinforce concrete walls?”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Yes, that’s what’s coming out of the victim’s mouth.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](I think I saw this in an old movie once...)[%p]
It was an old 80s horror flick that he’d seen with some friends in college.[%p]
The movie was about a bunch of travelers who got lost in the jungle and eaten by cannibals. In one scene, someone had been cooked like this.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
And there was one more thing.[%p]
The horrific scene in front of him called to mind a number of other incidents he’d seen in recent times.[%p]
Indeed. It was without question that he was reminded of the Return of the New Gen Madness.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](Is this another one?)[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](No, it’s not the 23rd anymore.)[%p]
On October 23rd, the killer had spent the whole day going after Miyashiro Takuru and his friends. Which meant the target had to be one of them.[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Shinjo-san?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Oh, sorry. Continue.”[%p]
Shinjo put the sheet back on the body again, and made another short prayer.[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“While we haven’t been able to identify the victim yet, I think it’s safe to assume that she’s the resident of this apartment.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Haida Riko-san, right?”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“That’s right.”[%p]
He flipped through his notebook and relayed to Shinjo what he’d learned from the condo’s manager.[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Eighteen years old. Single, lived alone. Supposedly she worked for a tech support company out of a temp agency, but when I tried to call the agency, they didn’t exist.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Either they went out of business, or it’s a front company for a prostitution ring.”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“We’re looking into it now.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“So what makes you think she’s the victim?”[%p]
Shinjo took out his own notebook and started to copy down the things he thought were important.[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Well, for one thing, there’s no sign of anyone but her coming to this room ever since she moved in.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“I’m sorry?”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Forensics tells me that all the fingerprints and footprints in the room probably belong to her, and there’s no sign that anyone’s wiped them away.”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Of course, there are some other fingerprints, but they’re all old. They probably belong to the former occupants, or a maintenance worker. Forensics thinks they can be ruled out.”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“The only shoe marks at the entrance belong to her, too.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“When we found the body, both doors were locked from the inside. And the windows were locked, too, which means it has to be her.”[%p]
Shinjo felt a little like he was going to faint.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](So how did the killer get in here and do this?)[%p]
It would take some serious equipment to roast an entire person. You couldn’t get that in here without leaving any traces.[%p]
The younger detective must’ve been thinking the same thing.[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“If she’d died in a more normal way, we could just write it off as a suicide without having to bother you, but...”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“There is no normal way to die.”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“I’m sorry, sir.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“There’s a security camera on the first floor, right?”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Yes, one of my men is looking at the footage in the manager’s room right now, but hasn’t found anything yet.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“I see...”[%p]
Shinjo looked at the victim, and then at the forensics people. They were frowning as they talked.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“From the look of it, we won’t be able to determine a time of death.”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Correct. Not without an autopsy. If the smoke alarms went off, we would’ve known right away, but...”[%p]
The two of them looked up at the useless devices on the ceiling.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“They’re broken, I’m told?”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Correct.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Who broke them? No, if hers are the only fingerprints in here, then it has to be her, right?”[%p]
But why would she do that?[%p]
Shinjo couldn’t make any sense of it.[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Anyway, Shinjo-san, I’ve never seen someone burnt to death before... but do they normally burn like this?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“No idea. I’ve never seen one, either.”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“The forensics people all say they can’t figure it out.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Even gasoline couldn’t do this.”[%p]
And there was no smell of gasoline, anyway.[%p]
The only stench here was that of burning meat.[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“I saw this on TV once, but—no, never mind.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Say it.”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“You won’t get mad?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Depends on what you say.”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“It was a special on psychic phenomena around the world. One section was on spontaneous human combustion—”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Stop it, fool.”[%p]
Shinjo poked the younger detective’s head, lightly.[%p]
The detective apologized, but Shinjo hadn’t stopped him because it was too unbelievable to be true.[%p]
He’d stopped him for the opposite reason: he was probably right.[%p]
Which meant he didn’t want rumors flying around, especially within the police department.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](“Pyrokinesis,” I think they called it.)[%p]
Kunosato Mio, Arimura Hinae, and Miyashiro Takuru had all used that word repeatedly.[%p]
It was the power to set things on fire with your mind, and they said the woman that was after Miyashiro and his friends—Minamisawa Senri—had it.[%p]
Was this Haida Riko woman attacked by a pyrokinetic, then? He came back to the same conclusion he’d reached earlier, but then soon shook his head.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](That doesn’t explain it, either.)[%p]
How did she get the rebar used to skewer the woman inside the room, and how did she actually skewer the woman herself?[%p]
Shinjo looked at the large window that dominated the room’s east side.[%p]
The curtain was open, and he could see the chaotic city of Shibuya outside.[%p]
If the building was taller, the view would be impressive, but from this height you could see the dirty backsides of the surrounding condos and multipurpose buildings.[%p]
It was hard to call it a nice view.[%p]
“That probably made the rent a lot cheaper, though,” he thought to himself, as he began to examine the glass window.[%p]
If the killer had used some kind of power to get the rebar in from the building across the street, this window would’ve been shattered.[%p]
But there wasn’t a scratch on it.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](I don’t think I’m going to get anywhere by myself here.)[%p]
He hated to say it, but he needed a specialist like Kunosato Mio. So for now, he decided to focus on gathering information.[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Shinjo-san.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“What?”[%p]
A light tap on his back brought him back to reality.[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“I got word from the guy looking at the security camera footage. He found a clear shot of the victim’s face.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“He did?! Let’s go!”[%p]
When he finally got out of the room, he felt like he could breathe again. Disrespectful to the dead or not, he took a deep breath when he got outside.[%p]
He went down to the first floor, and looked at the video on the monitor in the manager’s room.[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“He says Haida Riko-san always hid her face from the camera.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Was there some reason she didn’t want to be videotaped?”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Her face got burned during the Shibuya Earthquake, he said. And she hated people seeing her.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Oh, so that’s why.”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“But this time, he happened to get a glimpse of her.”[%p]
The timestamp on the screen indicated it was late at night on the 12th of October.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](October 12th? Isn’t that...)[%p]
He remembered writing that day down, so he flipped open his notebook.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](Oh, right. That’s the first day Miyashiro-kun and Arimura-kun were attacked by Minamisawa Senri.)[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Here’s Haida Riko-san, sir.”[%p]
He quickly turned his eye back to the screen.[%p]
In the video, the door to the entrance opened and a woman came staggering inside.[%p]
Perhaps her leg didn’t work right, or maybe she was injured, because he could tell she was dragging it.[%p]
Her hair covered her face, making it impossible to read her expression.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“W-Wait, is that...”[%p]
[name]Detective[line]“Yeah.”[%p]
Shinjo and the other detective gasped.[%p]
Haida Riko was holding a long object in her hands, something like a staff, and using it to support herself.[%p]
It looked exactly like the iron rebar that was currently skewering her body.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Wait... she was skewered on rebar that she brought in herself?”[%p]
It was just a recording, but Shinjo found himself holding his breath nervously, as if he was watching live footage.[%p]
She walked right by the security camera.[%p]
It did seem like she wanted to avoid showing her face.[%p]
However...[%p]
She must’ve tripped on something when she was right under the camera, because she suddenly staggered.[%p]
When she did, her hair flew backward, and he could finally get a glimpse of her face.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...!”[%p]
Shinjo’s eyes went wide.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“It... It looks just like her...”[%p]
All of her features matched the description.[%p]
They were identical to the composite sketch based on a description by someone who had barely survived an attack by this woman.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“N-No way... This is...”[%p]
The girl in the video was...[%p]
