Seven hours had passed since the beginning of the festivities, and even as the sun began to set, they showed no signs of slowing down.[%p]
Lines had started forming in front of every food stand and restaurant around lunchtime, and they were all still packed.[%p]
There were over 30 concerts, from huge thousand-seat venues with major artists, to tiny little bars with only a dozen or so seats. Each one was standing room only.[%p]
Guerrilla performance artists would approach the crowds, hoping to attract customers, and in turn attract crowds of their own.[%p]
Over 80,000 people were still in attendance at the festival, which had gone far beyond the organizers’ wildest expectations.[%p]
There were the usual issues associated with big events, like minor fights and lost children, but there had been no major problems so far.[%p]
The police from Shibuya Station helped to keep things safe, but the biggest factor was the volunteer staff.[%p]
The organizers had been recruiting volunteers up to the last minute to handle the crowds, keeping everyone safe and in a mood to spend money.[%p]
Many of the volunteers were victims of the original disaster, and the experience they’d gained in the earthquake had taught them how to get along and work with other people.[%p]
When a major band had made a surprise appearance at the stage in front of the 107 building, there was a mild panic, but the volunteers formed a human wall to keep people in the crowd safe.[%p]
[name]Man[line]“Today’s a day to celebrate the restoration. We can’t have anybody trampled to death.”[%p]
That’s what the leader of the volunteers had said to an interviewer who’d happened to be at the scene.[%p]
He was an honest and sincere man.[%p]
He was speaking from the heart, and he didn’t mean anything more than what he’d said.[%p]
But his words brought to mind a certain name, and a certain face, both among the people who saw him on TV and among those who heard him directly.[%p]
The word that had made them think of that name was, of course, “death.”[%p]
[name]Woman[line]“...What’s with those guys?”[%p]
[name]Man[line]“Those are the guys the news has been talking about all day.”[%p]
Everyone there was hoping for the festival to succeed.[%p]
But at the same time...[%p]
They hadn’t caused a big problem yet, but there was one big concern on everyone’s minds.[%p]
When a girl and her boyfriend left Shibuya Station, heading for the stage at the Cultural Hall, they saw a group of people wearing creepy masks.[%p]
There were over a dozen of them—and then another group came from another direction. Suddenly, there were several dozen of them.[%p]
Identical masks. Identical t-shirts.[%p]
The faces of two fat men, overlapping.[%p]
It was...[%p]
...the Sumo Stickers.[%p]
[name]Man[line]“...”[%p]
[name]Man[line]“...”[%p]
They said nothing.[%p]
But anyone who got close, whether by accident or because they wanted to get a picture, would flinch away as they heard the eerie sound created as the Sumos breathed underneath their masks.[%p]
The man at the head of the group looked around, and then approached a nearby street light.[%p]
[name]Man[line]“...”[%p]
Then he took something out of his pocket and stuck it on the light.[%p]
It was a Sumo Sticker.[%p]
The owner of a nearby crepe stall saw this and frowned, but was too scared by their appearance to say anything. He quickly went back to dealing with his customer.[%p]
It was something he’d seen again and again since the festival started.[%p]
The only ones who saw it were people like him, who stood in one place the whole day as they ran their businesses. The majority of attendees, caught up in the flowing crowds, saw nothing.[%p]
But...[%p]
If you were to take all the stickers placed on the ground, on the decorations, on the street lights, on the walls... You’d have enough to cover the huge TVs at Shibuya Scramble Crossing.[%p]
The stickers were slowly taking over, spreading even to places where no one could see them. They were like a physical manifestation of all negative emotions that lurked within the attendees.[%p]
It was about three hours later, around 7:30 PM, when they finally exploded.[%p]
It happened at the place that had been set up for the silent prayer that would end the festival...[%p]
...right in front of the memorial.[%p]
[name]Man[line]“I can’t see! Move!”[%p]

The couple a few feet away glanced over at her, but quickly turned away.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...”[%p]
Serika closed her eyes so she could concentrate only on the thoughts around her.[%p]
As of 9:00 PM, all the concerts and street performances, as well as a few of the stalls, were closing up.[%p]
The festival was nearing its end.[%p]
After this, the attendees would gather at the specified time for a moment of silent prayer, and then the festival would be over.[%p]
The crowd seemed to be heading toward the station and the memorial.[%p]
There were still too many people here for them to all fit in the memorial plaza.[%p]
So the big VIPs, as well as the families of the victims of the earthquake, would gather at the memorial itself, while everyone else would watch the proceedings on the TVs in front of the station.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line](Sakuma probably plans to reveal Miyashiro’s body at the memorial after he kills him, doesn’t he?)[%p]
Both the TVs in front of the station and the Memorial Park were near the Hikariwo, but the park was just a little closer.[%p]
And anything that happened at the park would be replayed for people watching on TV.[%p]
In other words, more people would see it.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line](That man from before was probably part of his plan...)[%p]

But she’d quickly learned that the report was mistaken.[%p]
It was a fake, a man pretending to be Miyashiro, who worshiped him as a “messiah.” But the man himself had looked nothing like Miyashiro.[%p]
Yet for some reason, the media had decided to report it as Miyashiro Takuru himself appearing at the memorial. As a result, security around the park and plaza had been strengthened.[%p]
And as a result of that, another rumor was spreading, this time that Miyashiro would be appearing there.[%p]
...It was almost certainly part of Sakuma’s plot.[%p]
