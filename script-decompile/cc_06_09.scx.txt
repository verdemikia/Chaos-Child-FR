[center][margin top="242"]October 12th, 2015. Monday.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
Shinjo looked up at the huge white building and sighed loudly.[%p]
AH Tokyo General Hospital.[%p]
After Kurusu’s report, he’d decided to visit it himself.[%p]
It was almost midnight.[%p]
Visiting hours were long over, and he didn’t have an official warrant. He was here entirely of his own volition.[%p]
But as he saw it, he was never getting a warrant the usual way, so this was his only shot.[%p]
Normally, he’d want to slowly, painstakingly build up a case, but it seemed to him that searching this hospital was so important that there just wasn’t time to spare.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](First I need to see if what Kunosato Mio said is true. If I can do that, I should be able to get as many warrants as I want.)[%p]
After his own investigations, he’d found that something strange had started happening here in 2007, two years before the earthquake.[%p]
At the time, there was a treatment facility in Shibuya called the “Ark Heart Medical Association” that was involved with some kind of cult.[%p]
On the surface, it was a mental health clinic staffed with talented doctors from university hospitals.[%p]
There was no way to know what they’d actually been doing, but evidently some people had suspected that they were brainwashing troubled patients.[%p]
But suddenly, the facility was reborn as a hospital with a non-profit assisted living facility.[%p]
And all the doctors that Ark Heart Medical had fired were rehired at AH Tokyo General Hospital.[%p]
And then six years ago, there was the New Generation Madness.[%p]
The fifth victim in that case...[%p]
Takashina Fumio, the victim of the so-called Numbskull case, who’d been kept alive for a week after his brain was removed...[%p]
...had worked at AH Tokyo General Hospital.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](What’s with all these creepy coincidences?)[%p]
There was too much that seemed suspicious.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](What would he have done at a time like this?)[%p]
And then he shook his head.[%p]
“Do it your own way.” That’s what he would’ve said.[%p]
Either way, Shinjo knew that if he could get proof of what was in this facility’s basement, the case would take a whole different turn.[%p]
Even if there was something big lurking in the shadows behind this case, if he presented incontrovertible proof, the police would have no choice but to act.[%p]
It wasn’t just about the facility, after all.[%p]
There were a lot of people involved, too.[%p]
The lives, and dignity, of the victims who’d been turned into test subjects also mattered.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](Experimenting on humans, huh? Disgusting.)[%p]
Every year, almost 100,000 people went missing.[%p]
If some of them had been forced into these experiments, that was a big problem.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Okay, let’s get this over with.”[%p]
His voice was somehow relaxed, yet driven, as he headed into the hospital.[%p]
He’d learned and memorized the location of the facility from Kunosato.[%p]
He snuck in from the ER loading zone and headed to the basement.[%p]
The temperature there was clearly different from the areas above.[%p]
And then he went to the autopsy room.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](If I remember right, this is where the entrance is.)[%p]
He opened the breaker box and saw the card reader inside.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](So this is it...)[%p]
He held out the card key that Kunosato had given him...[%p]
...and he heard the sound of an opening door.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](I see... so something big’s happening here.)[%p]
There was a hidden staircase in the back of the autopsy room.[%p]
A part of him hadn’t believed it until he’d seen it for himself... but now he was essentially sure.[%p]
A normal hospital wouldn’t need something like this.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Whew...”[%p]
Shinjo stopped for a moment to take a breath, then headed down the stairs.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
There was an old hallway at the bottom.[%p]
It smelled like mold and dust.[%p]
When he turned on his penlight, he saw that it was an old facility, and the walls were falling apart.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](But this place is still being used...)[%p]
If no one visits a building, it rots away.[%p]
Unless someone uses it every day, it won’t last long.[%p]
But this place was different.[%p]
It was old, but he was nearly certain someone was using it.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](I think she said it’s up this way.)[%p]
Shinjo followed the directions that Kunosato had given him as he headed toward his goal.[%p]
His first stop was the monitoring room, which would have critical information on the facility.[%p]
He wanted the data on the computer there.[%p]
And then he wanted to find the room where the victims of the experiments were being kept.[%p]
He turned down many corridors as he headed for his destination.[%p]
The cell-like rooms he saw on the way concerned him, but they could wait until later.[%p]
Right now, his biggest job was to get the evidence he needed.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](This must be the place...)[%p]
Shinjo finally found the spot he was looking for.[%p]
He used his card key to unlock the door.[%p]
Then he swallowed and put his hand on the doorknob.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
When he got inside, he saw—[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“What?!”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Damn it!”[%p]
He quickly ran to the other room, the prison where Yamazoe Uki had kept the test subjects.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line](You’re kidding me! This can’t be! It can’t be!)[%p]
He quickly found the room and flung open the door.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
And then he stood still in astonishment.[%p]
There was—[%p]
Nothing there.[%p]
No victims. No beds.[%p]
None of the things that Kunosato had told him about.[%p]
The monitoring room had been empty, too.[%p]
There were no security cameras, no computers...[%p]
Nothing at all.[%p]
All that remained was the simple fact that there was nothing left at all.[%p]
