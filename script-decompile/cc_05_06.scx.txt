The town was filled with noise that threatened to swallow her whole.[%p]
But that was just the way she wanted it.[%p]
Shibuya was a town that accepted anybody, and rejected everyone.[%p]
It was the only place that would let her be a part of it now.[%p]
She moved her legs forward, valiantly, amidst the hustle and bustle.[%p]
Forward, forward.[%p]
With each step she took, her face twisted with horrible pain.[%p]
Or at least, that’s how it seemed to her. She didn’t know if the pain was actually appearing on her face.[%p]
Her face was covered with scars, and shielded by long hair that hid her hatred.[%p]
But as she dragged her legs down the streets, no one in Shibuya stopped to look at her.[%p]
She was grateful for that...[%p]
...but at the same time, she hated it.[%p]
A flame burned within her womb.[%p]
It was a flame of hatred.[%p]
She was nothing but hatred now.[%p]
The businessmen who passed by her on the street,[%p]
the empty-headed students,[%p]
the happy couples,[%p]
the miserable-looking old people...[%p]
She hated all of them.[%p]
But she hated ‘them’ most of all.[%p]
She hated the ones who had abandoned her.[%p]
She hated the ones who’d done this to her.[%p]
If they hadn’t abandoned her, this never would’ve happened.[%p]
The hatred burned within her womb.[%p]
...No, she was the hatred.[%p]
The power she’d gained that day...[%p]
It was all for this.[%p]
Only the joy of revenge drove her forward.[%p]
Forward and forward.[%p]
And then...[%p]
...she didn’t notice that the people who passed by her turned back in surprise.[%p]
She didn’t realize it herself.[%p]
But this woman... was laughing.[%p]
She was laughing so hard that anyone could hear her.[%p]
Like black smoke from a flame rising toward the sky...[%p]
She raised her voice...[%p]
...and laughed.[%p]
