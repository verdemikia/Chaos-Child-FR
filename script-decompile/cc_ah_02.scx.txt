They say that every person has a single image, somewhere in their mind, that defines who they are.[%p]
For Arimura Hinae, that image was of her family, seated around a dinner table.[%p]
Her, her mom and dad, and her brother. The four of them sitting around the table.[%p]
That image, more than anything else, defined the kind of person that Hinae was.[%p]
Her mom and dad both worked, and on weekdays they’d often come home late. Sometimes they didn’t even come home at all.[%p]
But Hinae never felt alone.[%p]
She was never alone, because she had an older brother.[%p]
Her parents had told her that when she was born, he’d been the happiest boy in the world.[%p]
But she didn’t need to hear that to know how much she meant to her brother.[%p]
Hinae had always had a pretty face ever since she was a baby, and she’d always been popular.[%p]
But her brother loved her more than anyone.[%p]
Whenever Hinae cried, he’d always tried to make her feel better, and when it was time for her to sleep he’d rock her in his arms.[%p]
He was in one of the older classes at elementary school, and he’d probably wanted to play with his friends.[%p]
He’d had a lot of friends, actually, and every day they’d invite him over.[%p]
But he would always turn them down, and come straight home to be with Hinae.[%p]
With their parents working so much, he’d spent a lot of time alone in the house. He probably didn’t want Hinae to go through the same thing.[%p]
Normally, acting like that would have cost him his friends, but that had never happened. He was just that good of a big brother.[%p]
As Hinae got older, she became more and more like a young girl, and her brother loved her even more.[%p]
Her parents decided that Hinae could safely be left in her brother’s care, and started to come home even later.[%p]
But every Sunday, they were always home.[%p]
Sometimes, they’d all go out somewhere for the day, then come back in the evening to eat at the dinner table. That was the rule at the Arimura household.[%p]
There was always warm food, and her family was always smiling.[%p]
That was what happiness meant to her.[%p]
It was something so natural, and so normal, that she never doubted it.[%p]
And...[%p]
...it was the truth.[%p]
