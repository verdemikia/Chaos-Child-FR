[center][margin top="242"]October 10th, 2015. Saturday.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
Shinjo grimaced. The smell of death was enough to make him nauseous.[%p]
Watabe Tomoaki’s body had been taken to a university hospital and put on an autopsy table less than two hours after his death.[%p]
Normally, an autopsy would never be held this quickly.[%p]
There was paperwork that needed to be filled out, and the family needed to agree.[%p]
But Watabe’s death was so bizarre that a cause of death needed to be found as soon as possible.[%p]
Shinjo had taken a hard line with his superiors, and used rather forceful methods to get the family’s permission to perform a lightning-fast autopsy.[%p]
The police force as a whole seemed determined to avoid a repeat of the failures six years ago.[%p]
But what was truly unexpected was Kunosato’s statement that she would attend the autopsy as well.[%p]
Since he couldn’t let a civilian—especially a high school girl—attend an autopsy unsupervised, he’d been forced to go with her.[%p]
The hospital’s autopsy room was kept clean, and very different from the musty atmosphere you often saw in foreign movies.[%p]
But to Shinjo, the invisible smell of death was still thick in the air.[%p]
It was nothing like seeing a body at a crime scene.[%p]
It was almost physically uncomfortable.[%p]
But Kunosato Mio, as far as he could tell, didn’t seem bothered at all.[%p]
Shinjo and Kunosato were watching the autopsy from the waiting room next door. The wall had glass to allow them to see into the room.[%p]
The external and internal examinations had been completed, and now they were opening up the stomach and examining it.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Did you expect this to happen?”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Expect?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Why did you insist on going to see Watabe’s interview today?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Not only that, you ordered Arimura Hinae to make contact with Watabe yesterday.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Hmph.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Don’t tell me you’re actually the one behind all this, okay?”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“If I could do it, maybe I would.”[%p]
Shinjo couldn’t tell if she was serious or not.[%p]
He’d spent the last several days with her, but he still had no idea what she was thinking.[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“I did think that Watabe might be killed. I told Arimura to make contact with him so that I could be sure.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“But I certainly didn’t expect that he would be killed today.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“What would you have learned if Arimura Hinae had been able to make contact with Watabe?”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“...”[%p]
She didn’t answer him.[%p]
Even though he was sharing police information with her.[%p]
Would it really kill her to be a little more cooperative?[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Don’t you think Watabe landed too many big scoops in his short career?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Uh, yeah. What about it?”[%p]
Just as he spoke, he heard a muffled moan come from the coroner.[%p]
[name]Surgeon[line]“...!”[%p]
[name]Surgeon[line]“What the...”[%p]
There were microphones in the autopsy room that would let them hear anything said there.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“What’s wrong?”[%p]
[name]Surgeon[line]“I can’t believe this.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“What’s going on?”[%p]
Shinjo pounded on the glass and motioned for the coroner to explain.[%p]
The coroner nodded and moved the body so that they could see it better. Then he held up a pair of tweezers.[%p]
He plunged the tweezers into Watabe’s open abdomen.[%p]
From where Shinjo and Kunosato were standing, they couldn’t see inside.[%p]
The tweezers seemed to grab something.[%p]
[name]Surgeon[line]“Look at this.”[%p]
That was all he said as he lifted up the tweezers.[%p]
Between them was...[%p]
A Sumo Sticker, in excellent condition.[%p]
[name]Surgeon[line]“This was found in the stomach.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“It shouldn’t look like that.”[%p]
It wasn’t twisted or broken.[%p]
The sticker was almost entirely intact.[%p]
[name]Surgeon[line]“That’s not all.”[%p]
[name]Surgeon[line]“This is... insane.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“...”[%p]
Suddenly, Kunosato opened the door leading into the autopsy room.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Hey! You can’t just—”[%p]
You couldn’t go in there without changing your clothes or disinfecting yourself. He tried to tell her that, but gave up.[%p]
It was more important to find out what the coroner had seen.[%p]
So he followed her in, even though he knew he wasn’t supposed to.[%p]
The coroner said nothing.[%p]
He just stood there silently, holding his tweezers.[%p]
Even Kunosato was shocked by what she saw inside the body.[%p]
So was Shinjo.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“This is...”[%p]
He covered his mouth with a handkerchief.[%p]
It was just too bizarre.[%p]
Watabe’s stomach was so swollen, it seemed ready to burst.[%p]
And inside the stomach, he could see...[%p]
Not just dozens, but hundreds of Sumo Stickers.[%p]
[name]Surgeon[line]“We haven’t confirmed it yet, but the esophagus shows similar signs of swelling.”[%p]
[name]Surgeon[line]“There’s a good chance that it’s filled with stickers as well.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“What’s the most likely cause of death?”[%p]
[name]Surgeon[line]“...”[%p]
[name]Surgeon[line]“Probably suffocation.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“So is this what you’re telling me?”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Watabe swallowed sticker after sticker after sticker...”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“...until he eventually suffocated?”[%p]
No one, not Shinjo or the coroner, said a word.[%p]
