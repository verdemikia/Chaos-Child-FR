[center][margin top="242"]October 10th, 2015. Saturday.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
[center][margin top="242"]12:28 PM.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%p]
[center]In an unused classroom in Hekiho Academy.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
[name]Teacher[line]“Can you wait here? Sorry it’s so empty.”[%p]
[name]Watabe[line]“No, it’s my fault for coming early.”[%p]
Watabe bowed, and the man who had led him here from the teacher’s office bowed back. Then the man left the room.[%p]
He’d forgotten the man’s name as soon as he’d heard it. All he remembered was that he was a typical high school teacher, with somewhat delicate features.[%p]
That was all Watabe thought he needed to know.[%p]
[name]Watabe[line](Still... this place really is empty.)[%p]
He’d heard that Hekiho was famous, but once he’d gotten inside, he’d found that it was just a normal high school. Maybe a little cleaner than most.[%p]
He’d seen a TV crew from some station interviewing a student on his way here, but he couldn’t imagine there was anything worth asking one.[%p]
[name]Watabe[line](Well... I guess there are some people here who are worth something.)[%p]
Since his ostensible reason for being here was to do an article on the culture festival, he had to take a few pictures.[%p]
It didn’t matter what pictures he took, so he decided to just walk through the school. As he passed by a class that was doing some kind of Gero Froggy café, there was a bit of a fuss.[%p]
Student after student had asked him for handshakes and autographs. Almost the entire class had known his name.[%p]
He’d even taken a selfie with a girl who said she was a huge fan of his.[%p]
When he’d told her to keep it a secret, she’d given him an innocent smile.[%p]
That was the way it should be. That was the way normal people were supposed to act.[%p]
[name]Watabe[line]“I’m going to get even bigger.”[%p]
It was only a short while ago that an online friend of his had suggested he come here.[%p]
When he’d been putting together what he’d found out about the Sumo Stickers, those fucking brats at the Newspaper Club here had published just before he did.[%p]
He’d given up on publishing his own article, but at least decided to show it to someone. When he’d let his friend see it, they’d suggested he publish it anyway.[%p]
True, he was confident in the article itself. He’d gotten a very good picture of the Sumo Sticker at the love hotel, which the Newspaper Club had only been able to get from a distance.[%p]
There was a chance he’d be called out for plagiarism. But then his friend had continued...[%p]
[center][margin top="242"][color index="A0140000"]“You’re capable of turning that to your advantage, right?”[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
His friend knew him well.[%p]
[name]Watabe[line](At the end of the day, they’re still just a high school club.)[%p]
Everything would depend on how he handled the interview, but Watabe was confident he could use this to bolster his fame.[%p]
The ideal result would be to show that the Newspaper Club were the ones who had copied him.[%p]
They uploaded their newspaper to Niconiya all the time, so they were sure to know who he was. If he offered them the chance to help with his articles, they’d jump on it no matter what demands he made.[%p]
He’d started off at the very bottom, and he’d worked his way up this far.[%p]
He was capable of getting any scoop he wanted.[%p]
His power was a blessing from Heaven for a man who wanted to be a first-rate journalist.[%p]
In the whole history of journalism, there had never been a case where a net reporter had identified a suspect ahead of the cops, the news, or the magazines.[%p]
When a writer at a weekly magazine had found a suspect before the police, all of Japan was in an uproar. Watabe wanted to surpass that.[%p]
[name]Watabe[line](The only other thing I need for it to be perfect is connections with the police.)[%p]
His one worry was the love hotel incident.[%p]
When he’d charged into the crime scene and gotten into a fight with the cops there, his camera had gone out of focus and he’d failed to see part of the Sumo Sticker.[%p]
He’d used that to get this scoop, but if someone on the police force was paying attention to those stickers...[%p]
...and if they were enough of a weirdo to be paying careful attention to Watabe’s articles, they might find something strange.[%p]
[name]Watabe[line](Well, it’s probably not that different...)[%p]
People didn’t really want to hear the truth.[%p]
They wanted lies that sounded true, embellished so that they were all the more pleasing.[%p]
[name]Watabe[line]“Hm?”[%p]
When he heard the sound, he quickly flipped himself back into “hard-working, good-natured reporter” mode.[%p]
[name]Watabe[line]“Yes, come in.”[%p]
[name]Watabe[line]“Come in.”[%p]
They didn’t open the door. Nor did they say anything.[%p]
Watabe grunted, soft enough that they couldn’t hear, and then put on a smile as he went to answer the door.[%p]
