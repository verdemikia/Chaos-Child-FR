[center][margin top="242"]Three months later.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
Morning at the Aoba Dormitory group home.[%p]
[name]Yamazoe[line]“...Yuu?”[%p]
[name]Yuto[line]“I can do it on my own now.”[%p]
When Uki finished washing her face and came into the dining room, Yuto was already getting breakfast ready.[%p]
[name]Yamazoe[line]“I said I’d do it.”[%p]
[name]Yuto[line]“No, you can sit down.”[%p]
She looked inside the kitchen and saw that Yuto had already done all of Uki’s work for her.[%p]
“Fine. I’ll do the clean-up myself..”[%p]
As she thought that to herself, she sat in her chair without further complaint.[%p]
Lately, Uki had learned to make a few dishes without resorting to the recipe scrapbook that Kurusu and Yui had made.[%p]
Of course, her food didn’t taste quite as good.[%p]
But if nothing else, she wasn’t trying difficult recipes and burning the pan, like she’d been doing right after she came back here.[%p]
After a short wait, Yuto brought all the dishes to the table.[%p]
[name]Yamazoe[line]“Thanks. Okay, let’s eat.”[%p]
[name]Yuto[line]“Let’s eat.”[%p]
Yuto could also make simple dishes himself now.[%p]
It was his idea, and he’d been working hard at it.[%p]
Three months had passed after everything that had happened.[%p]
With its owner Sakuma, Takuru, Kurusu, and Yui gone, Aoba Dorm itself was in danger.[%p]
The place had only survived because of Sakuma’s salary, so that was natural.[%p]
But when Shinjo had told them it was safe to go back to Shibuya, they’d both insisted on coming home to Aoba Dorm.[%p]
Shinjo insisted it was impossible, but...[%p]
Surprisingly, it was Momose who helped them out.[%p]
She’d called in a few favors, and Aoba Clinic had become the property of a completely normal private doctor. And some of the clinic’s earnings were dedicated to keeping Aoba Dorm alive.[%p]
Of course, it wasn’t a family like it was before. The doctor just worked at the clinic. He didn’t manage the dorm.[%p]
Instead, a nutritionist and nurse, both of whom said they were acquaintances of Momose’s, would come by every day.[%p]
But Uki and Yuto wanted to keep as much of their old lives as possible, so they wanted to make their own food.[%p]
So on weekends and days when the nutritionist couldn’t come, the two of them eagerly went to the kitchen.[%p]
[name]Yamazoe[line]“Mmm. It’s good.”[%p]
[name]Yuto[line]“I cut it a little too thick, though...”[%p]
Only very recently had Yuto started to talk more.[%p]
After what had happened to Yui and Nono, and then what he’d been told about Sakuma and Takuru, he’d become nothing but an empty shell.[%p]
The one thing that had saved him was that even then, Uki had stayed by his side. Yuto had desperately wanted to be with someone after everything that happened.[%p]
[name]Yuto[line]“...”[%p]
Of course, the wounds in his heart hadn’t healed.[%p]
No, they probably never would.[%p]
The loneliness and regrets would come back to haunt him again and again.[%p]
Even Uki herself sometimes had trouble sleeping at night.[%p]
When that happened, she would go to Yuto’s room, and tell him it was because she knew he was afraid of being alone at night, and the two of them would endure the terror together.[%p]
The two of them were brother and sister, inseparable now.[%p]
Just like Nono and Takuru had been.[%p]
[name]Yuto[line]“...Oh, right... About the proposal...”[%p]
[name]Yamazoe[line]“Y-Yeah.”[%p]
Uki’s chopsticks suddenly froze.[%p]
[name]Yuto[line]“I’m okay with it.”[%p]
[name]Yamazoe[line]“Huh...?”[%p]
She didn’t expect that answer.[%p]
She’d thought Yuto would refuse.[%p]
[name]Yuto[line]“It would be better to have someone use them, I think.”[%p]
[name]Yamazoe[line]“...”[%p]
They were thinking of accepting new members at Aoba Dorm.[%p]
Which would mean they’d have to use Takuru or Nono’s rooms.[%p]
She’d wanted to save them, personally.[%p]
Nono wasn’t coming back, but she wanted to believe that Takuru might.[%p]
But if Yuto had made his decision, she had to support him as his big sister.[%p]
[name]Yamazoe[line]“...You’re right.”[%p]
She smiled the best she could, and started to eat more of her little brother’s cooking.[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“Ciao! It’s Hinae-chan♪”[%p]
The door opened, and Arimura Hinae walked into the Newspaper Club room.[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“Oh? You guys get free period too, Hana?”[%p]
[name]Kazuki[line]“Mmm.”[%p]
The owner of the room raised a hand in greeting without even looking up, like she always did.[%p]
[name]Arimura[line](Her mood is... good today.)[%p]
She’d learned how to tell from the sounds on the keyboard.[%p]
Arimura sat in her usual spot, and took out the book she was reading: Teru Miyake’s famous 6,000 Lives and Deaths.[%p]
There had been an awful lot of free periods lately, and during each one she would either come here and read a book, or work on a novel as part of her Literature Club activities.[%p]
Miyashiro, Kurusu, and Itou were gone, so Kazuki was always here alone.[%p]
And she almost never said anything.[%p]
There was no need to worry about hearing lies. It was the perfect place for Arimura.[%p]
After the incident, she’d spent several weeks in Akihabara before suddenly being allowed to go back to Shibuya.[%p]
...Though, “allowed” wasn’t the right word. She’d been dragged back here.[%p]
She hated it, but even if it meant another fight with her family, there was nothing else she could think of doing.[%p]
There hadn’t been any problems with psychics since then, at least publicly.[%p]
Sometimes Shinjo would call to check on her, but nothing special was going on.[%p]
But she’d never expected the case to end the way it had.[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“Oh...?”[%p]
She saw an unfamiliar expression in the book, so she decided to copy it down into the notebook she kept in the Newspaper Club.[%p]
Handwriting was the best way to do this stuff, she thought.[%p]
There was no class in just typing everything onto a computer.[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“Ummm...”[%p]
She stood up and looked for it on the steel shelves against the wall.[%p]
But it wasn’t where she thought it would be.[%p]
She reached out her hand toward the back of the shelf—[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“Oops...”[%p]
And the papers that had been piled there fell off.[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“Ah...”[%p]
[name]Kazuki[line]“...”[%p]
Arimura and Kazuki froze when they saw one of them.[%p]
It was a big map, folded many times over.[%p]
She didn’t have to spread it out to know what it was.[%p]
It was the one the Newspaper Club had put on the wall when they were going over the case.[%p]
But there was no need for it anymore.[%p]
So the first thing Kazuki had done after she’d come back to this room was take it off the wall.[%p]
Arimura had come back a few weeks later, but she hadn’t said a word about it vanishing.[%p]
No, it would be more accurate to say that she wouldn’t.[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“You know, come to think of it...”[%p]
[name]Kazuki[line]“Mmm...?”[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“They said Seri reacted to the light a little. They say it’s a sign that she might wake up.”[%p]
It was something that Shinjo had just told her on the phone.[%p]
[name]Kazuki[line]“Mmm...?”[%p]
Kazuki turned around to look at her. “What will you do?” her face seemed to say.[%p]
Arimura frowned and scratched her head.[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“I guess we’ll do exactly what Miyashiro-senpai wanted...”[%p]
There was nothing else she could do.[%p]
That was the promise she’d made to him.[%p]
[name]Kazuki[line]“Mmm...”[%p]
Kazuki nodded and looked somewhere else.[%p]
Arimura followed her gaze.[%p]
...She was looking at the chair that Miyashiro used to sit in. Then, she looked at the one Serika used to sit in.[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“...”[%p]
For what it was worth, Arimura hadn’t sat there a single time since she’d started coming here.[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“...Then Itou-kun’s completely innocent?”[%p]
That was Momose’s first question after hearing about what had just happened at the hospital.[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Correct. It seems he really was just being mind-controlled. It’s safe to assume he has no information on the Committee.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“The only other possibilities are Onoe and Miyashiro, but...”[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“...Not with the way things are looking, yeah.”[%p]
Kunosato and Momose both sighed at the same time.[%p]
Sakuma was dead.[%p]
They’d gone over everything he owned, as well as his body, but learned barely anything.[%p]
She had found—with the help of her very, very strange acquaintance in Akihabara—that Sakuma had indeed worked at the basement under AH Tokyo General Hospital. But nothing else.[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line](All the data was deleted...)[%p]
There was no data left of Sakuma.[%p]
She hated to admit it, but the Committee was good at their work, and fast.[%p]
Even the police didn’t seem to know who he was.[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“...”[%p]
That night, three months ago...[%p]
She still didn’t know what had happened between Miyashiro, Onoe, and Sakuma back then.[%p]
But if what she was imagining was correct, especially in light of her final call with Miyashiro, there was a need to question what Serika had told her at Freesia.[%p]
She’d talked a lot about the Committee, but looking back, there was a good chance it was a lie used to gain Kunosato’s cooperation.[%p]
As proof, even after she’d taken the psychics out of Shibuya, nothing had happened.[%p]
Her hacker acquaintance had checked his network, and hadn’t seen anything at all.[%p]
With no real reason to stay in Akihabara anymore, she’d come back to Shibuya... but while she was gone, all the threads connecting Sakuma to the Committee had vanished.[%p]
Either she’d screwed up...[%p]
Or maybe...[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line](Maybe Sakuma really was no one of importance after all.)[%p]
Which meant they simply gave up on him when he was no longer useful.[%p]
But there was a lot of equipment that had disappeared from that underground facility.[%p]
Even if he was the one who’d moved it... someone else had to be in charge of it by now.[%p]
If she could just find out who that someone was...[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“The other chance is...”[%p]
Momose looked at Kunosato and tapped her own temples.[%p]
[name]Momose[line]“In here. Chaos Child Syndrome.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“...You’re right.”[%p]
That was the one route she still had open to her.[%p]
It was the piece of evidence she’d chosen to use to get her revenge on the Committee.[%p]
That strange disease that had started showing up in certain victims of the earthquake—[%p]
Suddenly, her phone began to vibrate in her pocket.[%p]
She saw the name on the phone, and realized that it wasn’t time for his usual check-in. That meant something had happened.[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“What is it?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Onoe has—”[%p]
