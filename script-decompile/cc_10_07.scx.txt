[center][margin top="242"]At the same time.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
[center][margin top="242"]On the streets of Shibuya...[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
[name]Onoe[line]“...”[%p]
Serika was running. She was out of breath and had a terrible headache.[%p]
There was sweat all over her body, and it wasn’t from exhaustion.[%p]
Running a few minutes wasn’t enough to tire her out.[%p]
Compared to what she’d had to do to her mind and body to commit the murders, this was nothing at all.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“Damn...”[%p]
She touched the small cut on her stomach as she ran.[%p]
She felt slick blood, a sensation she was long since tired of.[%p]
She would probably have to stitch it up... but if her organs weren’t ruptured, she’d be fine.[%p]
She was used to getting stitches without anesthetic. She didn’t even need a towel to bite down on to relieve the pain.[%p]
The needle didn’t even need to be a medical one.[%p]
But this terrible headache was new to her.[%p]
Serika stopped when she saw there was no one around.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“You’re slowing me down...!”[%p]
She punched the wound on her stomach hard, two or three times.[%p]
The sensation of a fierce external pain, a sensation she was used to, ran up her spine.[%p]
She’d hoped that it would alleviate the pain of her headache.[%p]
She hoped that her brain would decide the pain of her open wound was more important than the headache.[%p]
But—[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“Tch...”[%p]
The headache didn’t stop.[%p]
It felt like a cold needle was being driven into her head with every heartbeat, as her mind screamed at her that something was terribly wrong.[%p]
She knew why.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line](Just trying to interfere with his power did this to me...?)[%p]
Her head hurt, but her mind was still capable of clarity.[%p]
Her foe was far stronger than she’d expected.[%p]
[center][margin top="242"]Mind control.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
An unimaginably horrible form of hypnosis, which could make anyone do whatever you wanted.[%p]
When he was an ally, she’d thought it was the greatest power imaginable. Now that he was an enemy, it seemed like the worst.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line](No...)[%p]
“That’s not right,” she laughed to herself.[%p]
He hadn’t been an ally.[%p]
He hadn’t been a helper or a co-conspirator, either.[%p]
If you could call him anything, he was a blackmailer.[%p]
The phone in her hand started vibrating.[%p]
It wasn’t the one she’d used when she pretended to be a normal person. She’d destroyed that a while ago, so that the police couldn’t track her.[%p]
It was the one he’d given her for communicating with him.[%p]
It probably belonged to someone they’d killed, or perhaps he’d used mind control to steal it from someone who never even knew it was gone.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...”[%p]
She stared at the screen for a moment, but finally decided to answer it.[%p]
[name]Man[line]“How’s your stomach?”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...”[%p]
[name]Man[line]“Sorry. You just attacked me out of nowhere, my body just moved on its own.”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“Liar. You were expecting it. I could read your mind.”[%p]
He must’ve found something funny, because the man on the other end laughed.[%p]
[name]Man[line]“That’s just like you. That’s the way kids should be, though.”[%p]
[name]Man[line]“So? You can at least tell me why, right?”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“You know damn well why!”[%p]
Serika’s voice was filled with rage.[%p]
He knew, and yet he had asked anyway.[%p]
He was either a sadist, or some kind of sick hedonist.[%p]
The methods he came up with for his murders always felt like one big joke.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“Why did you make Miyashiro Takuru out to be the killer? The plan was to make it look like Minamisawa Senri was behind this!”[%p]
[name]Man[line]“Hmm? Are you saying I was responsible?”[%p]
“Of course you were, asshole,” Serika thought to herself.[%p]
A warrant had been put out for Miyashiro only a few hours after Kurusu Nono’s death, and in a way that would normally never be possible.[%p]
There was no way they’d give out a minor’s real name in that short span of time, unless he had used his power.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“What the hell are you thinking?”[%p]
[name]Man[line]“He’s learned too much. I’m actually doing you a favor here.”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“What?”[%p]
[name]Man[line]“Your mind reading power has helped me a lot. I was supposed to kill him, but instead, I’m just going to have him arrested as the killer.”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...”[%p]
She hated the fact that she was on the phone.[%p]
Without being able to see him, she couldn’t read his mind.[%p]
She wanted to know what he was really thinking.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“It’s just better for your plans if he’s the criminal, not Minamisawa Senri, right?”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“He’ll be locked away, where he can’t tell anyone anything. And the press will think that he was the one who killed his sisters, and talk about the story even more.”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“That will attract even more attention to the Sumo Stickers.”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“And do you seriously expect me to believe he’ll get off with just an arrest? As of the first murder on September 7th, Miyashiro Takuru was eighteen years old.”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“There were seven victims. No minor has ever committed that many murders. The way public opinion’s going, there’s a 99% chance he’ll get the death penalty. Did you think I wouldn’t know that?”[%p]
[name]Man[line]“We won’t know until he goes to trial, will we?”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“And when that happens, you’ll be controlling the whole thing!”[%p]
Serika yelled into her phone, and the man started laughing again.[%p]
She didn’t know if it was because she’d yelled, or because his laughter pissed her off... but the headache got worse, which only made her even more angry.[%p]
[name]Man[line]“You really are great. Why not come back to me?”[%p]
[name]Man[line]“Your power’s of great value to me. And you’re smart. You have guts, too.”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“Shut the hell up. His safety was the one condition I had for helping you.”[%p]
[name]Man[line]“You’re also stubborn.”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“I don’t care about anything but that.”[%p]
Miyashiro Takuru’s survival.[%p]
That was the one thing she’d cared about since she came into this world.[%p]
Being a murderer, being an accomplice to murder, using her mind reading powers to find psychics—she’d done it all for that reason.[%p]
But now...![%p]
[name]Man[line]“So let me ask you, then. What will you do now?”[%p]
[name]Man[line]“You’re not going to take him and run, are you?”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...”[%p]
Serika managed to stop herself from speaking.[%p]
He was right. She’d thought it was the only way.[%p]
[name]Man[line]“That’s impossible, and you know it.”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“We won’t know until we try. Your power is a threat, but maybe there’s not much someone so low on the Committee’s totem pole can actually do, is there?”[%p]
That’s why he’d come to her for help, right?[%p]
She thought that this was her only chance to save Miyashiro.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“It’s a fact that as of right now, you don’t have him, and neither do the police. You don’t know where he is, do you?”[%p]
[name]Man[line]“You’ve got me there. That’s right. I’m all the way at the bottom of the org chart. I don’t even know who the main members are.”[%p]
And then he spoke in a voice that sounded a little too deliberate.[%p]
She could sense his lips curling into a smile.[%p]
[name]Man[line]“I guess Freesia would be a good place to start.”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...?!”[%p]
She finally gave a small gasp.[%p]
She’d thought that that’s where he was hiding, too.[%p]
How did he know about Freesia?[%p]
When she whispered that to herself, he said...[%p]
[name]Man[line]“Listen, they told me all about Freesia. Don’t you know how much he and Nono trusted me?”[%p]
