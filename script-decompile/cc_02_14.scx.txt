[center]The Shibuya Earthquake Memorial.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
The town was silent, as if it didn’t even know she was there.[%p]
She was standing before the names of the dead, as unmoving as the stone in front of her.[%p]
In fact, it was hard to tell that she wasn’t dead herself.[%p]
The wind that blew down from the tops of the buildings refused to touch her scraggly hair.[%p]
The eyes that poked out through her hair were cloudy and lifeless, and her lip was pressed into a frown. She must not have bathed recently, because the smell around her was terrible.[%p]
Her black clothes melted into the darkness, making the red jacket stand out a little against the gloom.[%p]
“...”[%p]
A sound came from her lips.[%p]
She pressed her jaw together as if trying to crush a piece of meat.[%p]
Her leg dragged along as she started to walk, her head facing the ground.[%p]
In that moment, she stood out from the scene around her.[%p]
The hair pressed against her face made it impossible to read her expression. But the most noticeable thing about her wasn’t the stench, or the sound of her gritting teeth. It was her anger and rage.[%p]
That anger and rage covered everything about her.[%p]
Her legs came to a stop.[%p]
She reached out a glove-covered hand and touched a name on the memorial.[%p]
She dug her fingernail into it.[%p]
She scratched again and again, as if trying to rub it out.[%p]
Her nail broke inside the glove, unable to handle the force.[%p]
But she didn’t stop.[%p]
She ignored the blood dribbling out of her glove, as if she didn’t feel any pain. Red continued to stain the memorial stone.[%p]
“...”[%p]
The date changed.[%p]
It was now October 9th.[%p]
