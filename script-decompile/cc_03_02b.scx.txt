[center][margin top="242"]October 9th, 2015. Friday.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
Tashita Hiroko was working at her part-time job as a grocery store cashier.[%p]
Her husband was always out working late, but he didn’t actually make any money. His air-headed personality meant that he was perennially stuck in lower management.[%p]
Her son was in college and spent all his time playing. She was spending a lot of money to send him there, but he’d grown into a selfish boy who constantly demanded cars and trips.[%p]
Of course, their budget was always stretched to the limit.[%p]
They hadn’t built up any kind of savings.[%p]
They lived in Shibuya, true, but she had to live with her in-laws, which didn’t make things easy at all.[%p]
The idea of going to lunch with her friends from the neighborhood seemed as remote as the fiction she saw paraded on the news every night.[%p]
She wanted an easier life.[%p]
Lately, she’d been all sighs.[%p]
Yesterday, she’d actually sighed at work, and gotten a warning from her boss.[%p]
The store where she worked was along Meiji-dori, about a five-minute walk from Shibuya. The lunchtime rush had ended and things were calming down.[%p]
She wanted to take a little break, but it wasn’t break time yet.[%p]
[name]Sales Clerk[line]“That will be 3,127 yen.”[%p]
The woman in front of her, who was taking out her wallet, was one she’d seen several times before.[%p]
She wore fancy clothes, and had a slender, attractive body. Her makeup was perfect, too.[%p]
Even when she was only going shopping at a supermarket.[%p]
She must live in a different world.[%p]
Maybe she was what they called a celebrity wife.[%p]
The woman seemed very anxious as she waited for her to finish.[%p]
Tashita hadn’t even handed over her change yet, and she’d already lifted up her shopping basket.[%p]
What was bothering her so much?[%p]
Was she feeling sick?[%p]
She did seem a little pale...[%p]
Tashita wondered about her, but they didn’t have enough of a relationship that she felt comfortable asking.[%p]
She handed her the receipt and her change.[%p]
And the woman quickly left.[%p]
Tashita greeted the next customer.[%p]
And then gasped.[%p]
It was a man.[%p]
And he was wearing a mask.[%p]
The mask had the same design as those things they called Sumo Stickers.[%p]
She’d heard about the stickers on the news several days ago. She’d learned that lately they were popular in Shibuya and nowhere else.[%p]
She had seen them several times on her way to and from work.[%p]
It was a disgusting design, and the idea of Shibuya being overrun with the things made her sick.[%p]
And this man was wearing a mask with the same design.[%p]
That meant she couldn’t see his face.[%p]
His clothes were completely normal. They weren’t particularly filthy, and he didn’t look like a pervert or a crazy man.[%p]
But that made it even stranger that he’d be wearing a mask to hide his face—and one so poorly-constructed that it was obviously handmade.[%p]
Was this why the last customer had seemed so anxious?[%p]
The man was standing completely still.[%p]
He didn’t say anything to Tashita.[%p]
And then she realized that she’d been staring at his face for quite a long time.[%p]
[name]Sales Clerk[line]“I... I’m sorry. Welcome.”[%p]
She looked inside his shopping basket.[%p]
It was filled with several bowls of instant ramen and a single two-liter.[%p]
[name]Sales Clerk[line]“That will be 4,378 yen.”[%p]
As she gave him the total, she happened to see the customers in the other lanes.[%p]
This time, she was struck completely silent.[%p]
It wasn’t just her register.[%p]
Every single register...[%p]
...had a customer wearing a mask.[%p]
There were so many of them.[%p]
And they were all standing silently.[%p]
There were already ten people lined up at her register, and all of them were wearing Sumo Sticker masks.[%p]
The store never got this busy, even at lunchtime on a Saturday.[%p]
The only thing the people in the masks had in common was that they were men.[%p]
They all wore different clothes.[%p]
A tall man in a sweatshirt.[%p]
A fat, middle-aged man in running clothes.[%p]
A well-built man in a sweater. And so on, and so on.[%p]
Not all of them looked like they had steady jobs, but not all of them looked like they were unemployed.[%p]
And not one of them said a word.[%p]
They just stood there in a line, masks on their head, and baskets in their hands.[%p]
The noisy supermarket suddenly fell silent.[%p]
All the other employees and customers stared at the men in the masks, eyes wide.[%p]
