[center][margin top="242"]October 9th, 2015. Friday.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
Shinjo took out a picture of the face of the man who’d stabbed Kurusu, then showed it to Kunosato.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Just like you guessed, the attacker was connected to that illustrator in the hospital.”[%p]
Kunosato didn’t really react.[%p]
She listened to him speak as if she knew everything he was going to say already.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“They were classmates in high school. At one point they were even making manga together, with one doing the art and the other doing the story.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Is there any chance these two were behind the murders? Do they have an alibi?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“The two of them haven’t left their rooms, and in the illustrator’s case, the hospital, for about a year now. We’ve got the hospital’s security camera records to back that up.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“As for the man who stabbed the Hekiho student, we have to rely on his family’s testimony, but when they arrived at the station—”[%p]
And then a phone rang. Kunosato picked it up without saying a word to Shinjo.[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“It’s me.”[%p]
Even from here, he could tell exactly how annoyed Arimura Hinae was.[%p]
But Kunosato cut her off before she could say a word.[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Are you calling to complain, or to give me your report?”[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“...”[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“That’s what I...”[%p]
Kunosato had no intention of listening to her complaints.[%p]
Did she do as she was told? Did she get any results?[%p]
That’s all Kunosato wanted to hear.[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Well?”[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“I told him. That’s what you wanted, right?”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“All right. You make sure you’re there tomorrow in person. Otherwise, there’s no point.”[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“Can you stop this? It’s really a pain in the neck, and I’m not interested in cooperating with you—”[%p]
Kunosato hung up before she could finish her sentence.[%p]
Shinjo was glaring at her.[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“What’s wrong? Continue.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“That was Arimura-san, right? Can you stop doing things without telling me first?”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Criticism that comes without an alternate plan is nothing but slander. And slander is how you prove you’re incompetent and have too much free time. Continue.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“You know...”[%p]
Shinjo frowned, dissatisfied, but gave up and flipped through the papers he was holding.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“How far had we gotten?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“The testimony from the family of the man who stabbed the Hekiho student.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Yeah. When they got to the station, the first thing they said wasn’t, ‘I can’t believe my son stabbed someone!’ It was, ‘I can’t believe my son left his room!’”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“...”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“They insist that he didn’t leave his room on the day of the murders.”[%p]
Kunosato looked upward for a second, as if lost in thought.[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“I think we can trust them. That crazy page he made was updated at the same time as one of the murders.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“It’s possible that time stamp was faked somehow, but... what did his psychological evaluation say?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“He was still going on about being the messiah, supposedly.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Megalomania, huh?”[%p]
Kunosato frowned as if she was annoyed.[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“What was the illustrator doing around 2007?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Hmm? 2007... They were still in Kyushu.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Is that certain?”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...? Yeah. They moved to Tokyo in late 2008, and ended up going to high school with the attacker.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“I see. So this one is a blind alley, then. All we ended up catching was a pair clinging to the barrier theory delusion.”[%p]
Suddenly she leaned back in her chair, seemingly bored.[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“So the two of them aren’t involved in the murders, huh? It’s likely they’re just copycatting the Sumo Stickers.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“Copycatting?”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Since they have alibis—even if they aren’t perfect—they’re unlikely to be connected to the case directly. The only indirect connection would be the Sumo Stickers...”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“If one did the writing, and one did the art, it was probably the professional illustrator who did the art for the manga they produced.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“The Sumo Stickers first appeared in Tokyo in 2007. At that time, they were in Kyushu.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Their manga didn’t work out, so they started claiming to be the creators of the Sumo Stickers out of a desire for approval. Then the two of them went crazy...”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“One’s artistic ability caused them to let the Sumo Stickers take complete control, and the other used that as fuel for his delusions, as if he was writing a story.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Of course, I have no evidence to prove this hypothesis, but we’ll know soon enough. Anyway...”[%p]
What was more concerning was the fact that the man hadn’t left his room for years. Would he really leave it just because he’d learned about a bunch of stickers appearing?[%p]
And...[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line](AH Tokyo General Hospital...)[%p]
That was where the illustrator was kept.[%p]
During the New Generation Madness, there were rumors that the hospital was involved with a certain religious organization that was connected to that particular case.[%p]
And it was also supposedly related to SafeWorks, the company involved in the restoration work that had resulted in security cameras all over town.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“I’m not sure I honestly believe that these stupid stickers are really involved in the case.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Then don’t worry. You’ll be working on something else tomorrow.”[%p]
Kunosato opened up a file she’d saved on her PC.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“What’s this?”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“It’s an article by Watabe, who’s having an interview with the Hekiho Newspaper Club tomorrow.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“The net reporter you secretly made contact with to start that flame war?”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Yeah.”[%p]
She nodded, and then opened up a picture of the photo of the Sumo Sticker that was taken at the scene. Then she lined it up against the one in the article.[%p]
Shinjo stared at it for a moment... and then he realized.[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“The expressions are different?”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Watabe’s made a name for himself by sneaking into a lot of different crime scenes, not just this one, and getting pictures.”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“There were so many of these pictures that some people started saying he was faking them. Watabe happily agreed to hand them over to a specialist, and...”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“There was no sign of the photos being altered.”[%p]
[name]Shinjo[line]“...”[%p]
Shinjo figured out what she was trying to say. “No way,” he whispered to himself.[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“I’m going to have Arimura find out tomorrow. If I’m right...”[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“That culture festival might be a lot of fun.”[%p]
