[center][margin top="242"]November 4th, 2015. Wednesday.[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
The roof was normally a place for students to laugh and talk.[%p]
But under the cold moonlight, the empty benches seemed silent and sad.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“...”[%p]
Kurusu looked at the benches and shivered once more.[%p]
It wasn’t just because of the cold. It was because she had realized that this place was truly empty.[%p]
Of course it was. No one was allowed to be here at this hour.[%p]
It was an isolated place, where the warm light didn’t reach. Only the cold light did.[%p]
No matter what happened here, the rest of the world wouldn’t know. It wouldn’t exist outside of this place.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line](It almost seems too perfect.)[%p]
It was so perfect for her that it was almost sad, Kurusu thought.[%p]
While Kurusu was waiting for her to arrive, she hadn’t been thinking about the case... strangely, she’d been thinking about her family and friends instead.[%p]
What had she always said to her family before she’d left to come here in the morning? Did they smile?[%p]
The younger students who’d helped with the student council’s work...[%p]
The talks she’d had with her classmates about the latest fads...[%p]
The brief fights in the lunch room, forgotten as soon as the bell rang to tell them to go back to class.[%p]
Each time she started to lapse too deep into her memories, a cold wind would blow and pull her back into reality.[%p]
And then she’d realized something, with a self-abasing laugh.[%p]
She knew it.[%p]
The reason Nono wasn’t thinking about the case was because that part of her was sure she already knew everything.[%p]
If she asked, she would get the fatal answer that would lay bare the truth.[%p]
And once she finally heard the answer...[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“...”[%p]
A strong wind blew, as if to carry away her thoughts, and she closed her eyes for a moment.[%p]
And when she opened them, she heard the sound of a rusty door opening.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“Non-chan?”[%p]
Kurusu’s lips twisted a little when she heard her say that.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line](Non-chan, huh?)[%p]
It made her mad to hear herself called that.[%p]
She felt heat rising from the bottom of her stomach. It was time to begin.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“I’m really sorry to bring you out here so late.”[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“...Hey, Onoe?”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...Non-chan?”[%p]
Did she realize that Kurusu was addressing her by last name now?[%p]
Or did she sense something instinctively?[%p]
Serika repeated herself, this time with a suspicious look on her face.[%p]
She tried to get closer, but a glance from Kurusu stopped her.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...”[%p]
Serika froze.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“Answer me. Was it you?”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“Was what me?”[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“Answer me.”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...?”[%p]
Kurusu repeated her words to her former best friend, the one person outside her family that she called by first name.[%p]
But Serika’s expression didn’t change.[%p]
It was the same face Kurusu knew so well.[%p]
The same look that Serika wore when she bought a crepe and started wondering what was inside.[%p]
Thinking back, the two of them went shopping together all the time.[%p]
Onoe had insisted that she looked fine without makeup, and Kurusu had insisted on buying her some anyway... It had been a lot of fun.[%p]
And they were always together in the club.[%p]
She’d argued against Takuru’s reckless plans, stopped Kazuki from pounding on the walls, and the two of them were always amazed at the stupid things Itou-kun said.[%p]
They’d always been laughing together...[%p]
But now...[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“You were there at the scene of the first murder, weren’t you?”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...? I don’t know what you’re talking about, Non-chan. Where is this coming from?”[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“There was a sound in the video. A sound I knew very well.”[%p]
In the Don’t Look video...[%p]
She’d heard that strange sound that Serika’s Gero Froggy always made.[%p]
Serika had a bad habit of unconsciously fiddling with it whenever she was focusing on something.[%p]
Kurusu took a step forward to stop herself from falling over...[%p]
And glared at Serika with a challenge in her eyes.[%p]
That’s right. Kurusu was glaring at Serika.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“W-Wait, Non-chan—”[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“I told you to answer me!”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...!”[%p]
Serika staggered back at the sound of her loud footsteps.[%p]
Finally, her expression changed.[%p]
There was fear in it.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“Our family lost Yui. She’s gone. And she’s never coming back.”[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“Why... Why did you take her away from us?”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...”[%p]
Serika stayed quiet, that terrified expression still on her face.[%p]
Was she really terrified by Kurusu?[%p]
Or was she trying to fool her?[%p]
Or...[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“...”[%p]
Kurusu took another step forward.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“On that day, eight years ago, did you really go down into that hospital basement? Were you really there with Takuru?”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...”[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“You can’t answer me, can you? Because you weren’t actually there. I know that better than anyone.”[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“So... who are you?”[%p]
Kurusu’s question was something she’d wondered about ever since Onoe had said she’d gone into that basement with him.[%p]
It was the one last thing she’d been unsure of, which she’d kept quiet about to protect her own secret.[%p]
Until a little while ago, it had seemed like it didn’t matter.[%p]
She was willing to go her whole life without finding out...[%p]
...because Onoe Serika was someone she loved.[%p]
But now—[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“Tell me. Who are you?”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...”[%p]
But Serika said nothing. Not a word.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“...”[%p]
Did she really not know anything?[%p]
The thought crossed her mind for a moment, but she quickly kicked it away.[%p]
That was just what she wanted to believe.[%p]
She’d had suspicions about Serika for a long time.[%p]
But she’d ignored them because she was too afraid to ask.[%p]
And the result of that had been Yui’s death.[%p]
Kurusu raised her hands and brought them together, as if offering up a prayer.[%p]
And the next moment...[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“...!”[%p]
She brought them down, hard and straight, as if swinging a sword.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...!”[%p]
Serika moved back, just a little.[%p]
Her movement might have looked like pure reflex, but Kurusu didn’t miss it.[%p]
Which meant...[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“So... I was right, huh?”[%p]
...That was the answer to everything.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“So... that was it, huh?”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...”[%p]
Serika’s face became blank and expressionless.[%p]
She didn’t frown or sigh at her own failure. Her manner became that of a beautiful doll.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“Serika...”[%p]
She was right.[%p]
She’d been ready for it, but she still couldn’t help but cry.[%p]
She tried to stop herself.[%p]
She gritted her teeth.[%p]
But Serika’s gaze wasn’t fixed on her anymore.[%p]
She stood, legs apart, as if judging the distance between herself and the “thing” in the seemingly empty space between them.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line](No question about it...)[%p]
She could see what Kurusu was holding.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...”[%p]
And now, it was Serika’s turn to look into her eyes.[%p]
Her eyes were different than Kurusu had ever seen before. It was as if they saw everything.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line](No, that’s not right!)[%p]
Suddenly, Kurusu realized.[%p]
It wasn’t just a simile.[%p]
Her eyes really did see everything.[%p]
It was... telepathy.[%p]
A terrible, twisted power, which could expose all of your most treasured secrets![%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“Aah...!”[%p]
Kurusu heard the sound of herself clenching her jaw.[%p]
And then she forgot the tears, and turned herself over to the rage that was boiling within her.[%p]
It had been six years since she’d realbooted it, and it was almost sad how easily it came into her hand.[%p]
A DI-sword...[%p]
A monstrous blade that only those with the power to transform delusions into reality could wield.[%p]
She refused to look away from the light that she’d never wanted to see again as she pointed the blade at her opponent.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“You were the one using Minamisawa Senri, weren’t you?!”[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...I don’t want to hear that from you of all people, Kurusu Nono.”[%p]
Her voice made Kurusu shiver.[%p]
And a blade even more wicked than her voice appeared in Serika’s hands, opening like a blooming, poisonous flower.[%p]
It was beautiful.[%p]
For a moment, Kurusu was blinded by the beauty of the weapon.[%p]
She forgot that it threatened to cut into her soft flesh.[%p]
[name]Onoe[line]“...”[%p]
Serika leapt off the ground.[%p]
She closed the distance between them in an instant.[%p]
Kurusu brought down her own sword as hard as she could, without even time to follow her movements.[%p]
[name]Kurusu[line]“...! UWAAAAAAAAAAAH!”[%p]
And as she screamed...[%p]
[center][margin top="242"][name]Kurusu[line]“I’m sorry.”[evaluate expr="291EA4B5281400811400"][%e]
...she heard her own voice.[%p]
By the time she realized she was talking to Takuru, it was already too late—[%p]
The light flashed in front of her, as if driving the thought from her mind.[%p]
