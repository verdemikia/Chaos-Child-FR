Her mother and father were smiling gently, like always.[%p]
The cozy atmosphere hadn’t changed at all.[%p]
But Hinae knew now that it was all a lie.[%p]
It was only by chance she’d found out.[%p]
On her way back from a school trip, the bus had been stopped at a light. She’d looked out the window and seen someone who looked like her mom.[%p]
At first, she thought she must be mistaken. She had to be wrong, because the woman that looked like her mom was walking down the street arm in arm with a young man.[%p]
She looked again and again to make sure that she was wrong.[%p]
But the more she looked, the more certain she became that she was right.[%p]
Hinae wasn’t young enough anymore to not know what that meant.[%p]
She wasn’t sure if she should tell her dad.[%p]
If Mom was lying to Dad—if Dad was smiling while she lied to him, then that was just horrible for him.[%p]
That was what Hinae thought to herself. But she was wrong.[%p]
Her father was cheating on her mother, too.[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“Big Bro, there’s something I want to talk to you about...”[%p]
[name]Shingo[line]“Hina? What’s up?”[%p]
Hinae was still too young to keep her parents’ problems to herself.[%p]
The only person she could rely on was her big brother Shingo.[%p]
She told her brother everything.[%p]
About Mom. About Dad.[%p]
Of course, he’d be shocked... at least, that’s what she thought. So she was totally surprised by his next words.[%p]
[name]Shingo[line]“I see... So you found out too...”[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“Huh?”[%p]
Her brother knew.[%p]
Her brother was the one who taught her not to lie, but he’d been lying to her, too.[%p]
It felt like a terrible betrayal to her.[%p]
Everyone was lying.[%p]
Everything around her was built on a lie.[%p]
Hinae wanted the truth.[%p]
But what exactly was “truth”?[%p]
She was too young to know.[%p]
But there was one thing she did know.[%p]
Her brother had been hurt, too.[%p]
He’d been suffering alone, keeping a secret that he couldn’t share with anyone.[%p]
From that day on, the only truth she had was her bond with her brother.[%p]
They were siblings, sharing the same pain.[%p]
That bond was what kept her going.[%p]
Hinae became even more dependent on her brother.[%p]
And her brother responded by coming to love her even more.[%p]
When Hinae was just a year or two from graduating elementary school, her brother said to her...[%p]
[name]Shingo[line]“Once I graduate, I’m leaving this house. You should come with me.”[%p]
From that day on, those words were what kept Hinae going.[%p]
Her bond with her brother, and her hopes of escaping her parents’ lies.[%p]
And that became her new future, and her truth.[%p]
But six years ago, on that day, that dream was destroyed.[%p]
There was a dazzling light, and a great shaking—and she found herself buried in the rubble.[%p]
