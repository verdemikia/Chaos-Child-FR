[name]Arimura[line]“I just know that she was Miyashiro-senpai’s childhood friend. Nothing else.”[%p]

And all they’d ever talked about was the case. She didn’t know much at all about Serika’s private life.[%p]
[name]Kunosato[line]“Did she ever lie about her past? It doesn’t matter how small it was.”[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“I don’t think so. Seri— That girl didn’t lie very much.”[%p]
That was the truth.[%p]
Arimura was capable of recognizing even common courtesies and jokes as lies.[%p]
That’s why she knew how filled with lies most people’s conversations were.[%p]
And that was the real reason why everything she said at school was a joke.[%p]
Nothing made her more upset than when she was talking about something serious, and the person she was speaking with responded with a lie.[%p]
That’s why she told jokes. Everyone around her joked back.[%p]
It was a lot easier that way.[%p]
But out of all the people she knew, Itou and Serika from the Newspaper Club were some of the ones who lied the least.[%p]
They just weren’t any good at it.[%p]
But to Arimura, that was a greater blessing than the others could imagine.[%p]
[name]Arimura[line]“...”[%p]
That was why it had been such a shock.[%p]
It might not have been as bad for her as it was for Miyashiro, but she felt like she’d been betrayed by someone who was more than a friend.[%p]
